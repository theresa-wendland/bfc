5 Tips to Survive a Coding Bootcamp

Theresa Campbell
September 1,2020

Follow Us

I attended Nucamp’s 17-week long Front End and Mobile Development Bootcamp. The experience propelled my web development career. Five months after graduating, I landed my first job as a Web Developer at Sagentic Web Design. During the 17 weeks, I created three portfolio projects, participated in countless coding challenges and in-person workshops. Here are five tips that gave me the upper hand. Going Ahead of Schedule My instructor sent weekly emails with a study calendar advising me what content to cover on specific days. The calendar was a useful guide, but I strived to be at least one day ahead of schedule. Going ahead of schedule gave me a buffer for the unexpected social events that popped up! Taking Notes Even if you don’t plan on reading them, taking notes forces you to slow down and digest the information. Notes will come in handy, especially on Saturday mornings, when you are called on to explain the difference between an arrow function and a function expression. Reaching Out Following the 20-minute rule will reduce your moments of frustration and save time. If you are stuck or feeling overwhelmed, reach out to your instructor or coursemates, they are your best resources. Remember, one of the reasons you enrolled in a Bootcamp was to have someone who will assist you. Networking Use your strengths to make some new connections. Start by offering or asking for assistance. Some of my classmates had years of experience and helped me understand many concepts, while others were still trying to figure out how to open a terminal, and I offered my assistance. Preparing for the in-person Workshop On Saturdays, we reviewed the week’s content, worked in groups, and submitted a weekly assessment. If your instructor is okay with you starting the assignment ahead of time, give it a shot! Getting a head start played a vital role in submitting my work on time. Pro-Tip: Revise the week’s content before the workshop. Chances are you won’t remember what you studied on Monday. Attending a Bootcamp is often a costly venture, which is why I chose Nucamp. Nucamp’s affordable pricing and flexibility made it easy. It is the only Bootcamp on the market in its price range, and classes are on Saturdays.
